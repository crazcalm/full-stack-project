Who, When, Where, When, and Why

Who am I?

My name is Marcus Willock, and I am person that dedicates themselves to learning. I gain pleasure from being a little bit smarter than I was yesterday. For a long while, my largest monthly expense was the purchasing of books. Later in life I learned that owning a ton of books while moving sucks! Since then, those books have either been replaced by digital copies or thrown out.

lifeExperience++;

What is the meaning of crazcalm?

For those who do not know, crazcalm is my online handle. Anyway, back in the day when I decided to create an AOL instant messenger account, I did not know what my username should be. Luckily, they had a username generator that took in three words and generate a list of possible names.

I choose the words "crazy", "calm", and I cannot recall the third word used. Anyway, crazcalm was one of the names that the generator gave me, and I have been using it ever since.

Where did you learn how to program?

I learned how to program at Rutgers University while studying to become a Chinese language teacher. Long story short, I have always been interested in programming, but, as an undergraduate, I did not have any free time to study it.

Hovever, once I got to Graduate school, I suddenly had more free time than I knew what to do with! I picked up programming, I joined the CS clubs on campus, I attended hackathons, and, eventually, I dropped out of my program to pursue a career in programming.

When was the first time you felt stupid?

Probably as a child. I have a hard time registering time. For example, one of my friends has two birthdays in my google calender, because I can never remember if her birthday is in the fall or the spring. So I send her a gift on for both birthdays.

Another example can be taken from my college years. Up until graduation, I always thought a semester was 3 months long...

Why do you program?

I program for a number of reason, but my initial reason was because programming fulfilled a promise that math could not. The lie that you are told in mathematics is that, "Once you prove something, you will never have to prove it again," which is not true for students because all of your proofs, which are written on paper, get thrown away at the end of class. With code, I can commit everything to my github and reuse it when needed!

Since then, I have a ton of reasons as to why I should program, but most of them boil down to the fact that I enjoy programming
